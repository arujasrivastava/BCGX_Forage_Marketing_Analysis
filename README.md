## 📋 Overview

This project analyzes 500 website visitors across three marketing channels (Email, Instagram, Web Banner) and two campaign types (A & B) to identify the most effective channel-campaign combinations for customer acquisition and retention.

**Dataset:** BCG X Forage - Data for Decision Makers Job Simulation (Week 1)  
**Time Period:** April 2026  
**Tools:** Power BI, Excel

---

## 🎯 Business Problem

A marketing team running concurrent campaigns across multiple channels needed answers to:
- Which channel generates the highest revenue?
- How do different customer segments (new vs. existing) respond to each channel?
- Which campaign-channel combination works best for acquisition vs. retention?
- What is the relationship between customer engagement and conversion quality?

**Objective:** Provide data-driven recommendations for budget allocation and campaign strategy.

---

## 📊 Key Findings

### **Finding 1: Email Dominates Revenue Generation**
- **Email generates $7,909 (54.6% of total revenue)**
- 3x higher revenue than Web Banner
- $6,749 combined from Instagram + Web Banner
- **Implication:** Email is the primary revenue driver and should receive the largest budget allocation

### **Finding 2: Email + Campaign B = Best New Customer Acquisition**
- **62.8% of new customers come from Email**
- **Campaign B converts 64.5%** of email's new customers
- Email's formal, professional tone builds trust with unfamiliar audiences
- **Recommendation:** Use Email + Campaign B for cold audience acquisition

### **Finding 3: Instagram + Campaign A = Best Existing Customer Retention**
- **51.9% of existing customers come from Instagram**
- **Campaign A converts 54.3%** of Instagram's existing customers
- Instagram's informal, community-driven experience resonates with familiar audiences
- **Recommendation:** Use Instagram + Campaign A for customer retention and loyalty

### **Finding 4: Email Drives Highest-Quality Conversions**
- **Email converters spend 120.24 seconds** on average
- Instagram converters: 89 seconds (-26%)
- Web Banner converters: 94 seconds (-22%)
- **Implication:** Email drives high-intent traffic with deeper engagement = higher purchase quality

## 🔍 Methodology

### Data Source
- **Dataset:** BCG X Forage Week 1 Campaign Data
- **Records:** 500 website interactions
- **Metrics:** Visitors, conversions, revenue, time on site, campaign type, channel, customer type

### Analysis Approach
1. **Descriptive Analysis:** Revenue, conversion rates, and engagement by channel and campaign
2. **Segmentation Analysis:** Performance variations by customer type (New vs. Existing)
3. **Engagement Analysis:** Correlation between time-on-site and conversion quality
4. **Strategic Synthesis:** Channel-campaign combinations and lifecycle recommendations

### Key Metrics Calculated
- **Conversion Rate:** Converted visitors ÷ total visitors
- **Average Order Value (AOV):** Total revenue ÷ converted visitors
- **Time Spent Category:** Engagement levels bucketed as Low (31-60s), Medium (61-120s), & High (120+ seconds) to identify correlation between engagement duration and conversion quality
